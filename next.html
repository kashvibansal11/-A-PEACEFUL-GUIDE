     Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being.
    It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, 
    and make healthy choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and 
    adolescence through adulthood.

Some lesser known facts about mental health...

  • The largest proportion of mental health issues are actually neither severe nor enduring but commonly mild to moderate short-lived mental health conditions.
  • Global prevalence figures show that depression affects an estimated 300 million people worldwide and anxiety disorders concern almost 4% of the world's population.

SOME METHODS TO IMPROVE MENTAL HEALTH

        
Sleep

        Sleep is a state of reduced mental and physical activity in which consciousness is altered
        and certain sensory activity is inhibited.During sleep, there is a marked decrease in muscle
        activity and interactions with the surrounding environment.
        
Talking too someone

        Talk to someone you trust. Opening up to someone you trust can help you feel listened to and 
        supported. It could be a friend, partner or family member.
        
        
Excercise

        Exercise is physical activity that enhances or maintains fitness and overall health.
        Regular exercise may improve depression or anxiety symptoms enough to make a big difference.
